Faecal microbiota transplantation halts progression of human new-onset type 1 diabetes in a randomised controlled trial

粪便微生物群移植在一项随机对照试验中阻止了人类新发1型糖尿病的进展

DOI: 10.1136/gutjnl-2020-322630

URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33106354/

Journal: Gut ||| Not finding IF

Date: 2021 Jan

Abstract: Objective: Type 1 diabetes (T1D) is characterised by islet autoimmunity and beta cell destruction. A gut microbiota-immunological interplay is involved in the pathophysiology of T1D. We studied microbiota-mediated effects on disease progression in patients with type 1 diabetes using faecal microbiota transplantation (FMT). Design: Patients with recent-onset (<6 weeks) T1D (18-30 years of age) were randomised into two groups to receive three autologous or allogenic (healthy donor) FMTs over a period of 4 months. Our primary endpoint was preservation of stimulated C peptide release assessed by mixed-meal tests during 12 months. Secondary outcome parameters were changes in glycaemic control, fasting plasma metabolites, T cell autoimmunity, small intestinal gene expression profile and intestinal microbiota composition. Results: Stimulated C peptide levels were significantly preserved in the autologous FMT group (n=10 subjects) compared with healthy donor FMT group (n=10 subjects) at 12 months. Small intestinal Prevotella was inversely related to residual beta cell function (r=-0.55, p=0.02), whereas plasma metabolites 1-arachidonoyl-GPC and 1-myristoyl-2-arachidonoyl-GPC levels linearly correlated with residual beta cell preservation (rho=0.56, p=0.01 and rho=0.46, p=0.042, respectively). Finally, baseline CD4 +CXCR3+T cell counts, levels of small intestinal Desulfovibrio piger and CCL22 and CCL5 gene expression in duodenal biopsies predicted preserved beta cell function following FMT irrespective of donor characteristics. Conclusion: FMT halts decline in endogenous insulin production in recently diagnosed patients with T1D in 12 months after disease onset. Several microbiota-derived plasma metabolites and bacterial strains were linked to preserved residual beta cell function. This study provides insight into the role of the intestinal gut microbiome in T1D. Trial registration number: NTR3697.

摘要: 目的：1型糖尿病（T1D）的特征是胰岛自身免疫和β细胞破坏。肠道微生物与免疫的相互作用与T1D的病理生理有关。我们使用粪便微生物群移植（FMT）研究了微生物群介导的对1型糖尿病患者疾病进展的影响。设计：将新近发病（<6周）T1D（18-30岁）的患者随机分为两组，在4个月内接受三个自体或异体（健康供体）FMT。我们的主要终点是在12个月内通过混合膳食测试评估保留受刺激的C肽释放。次要结果参数是血糖控制，空腹血浆代谢物，T细胞自身免疫性，小肠基因表达谱和肠道菌群组成的变化。结果：与健康供体FMT组（n=10个受试者）相比，自体FMT组（n=10个受试者）在12个月时C肽水平得到了显着的保护。小肠Prevotella与残留的β细胞功能成反比（r=-0.55，p=0.02），而血浆代谢产物1-花生四烯酸-GPC和1-肉豆蔻酰基-2-花生四烯酸-GPC的水平与残留β细胞的保存呈线性相关（rho=0.56，p=0.01，rho=0.46，p=0.042）。最后，十二指肠活检中的基线CD4+CXCR3+T细胞计数，小肠脱硫弧菌的水平以及CCL22和CCL5基因的表达预测了FMT后保留的β细胞功能，无论供体特征如何。结论：在发病后的12个月内，FMT阻止了新近诊断的T1D患者的内源性胰岛素产生下降。几种微生物来源的血浆代谢物和细菌菌株与保留的残留β细胞功能有关。这项研究提供了肠道肠道微生物组在T1D中的作用的见解。试用注册号：NTR3697。
